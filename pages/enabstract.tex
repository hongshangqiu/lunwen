% English Abstract

With the widespread adoption of cloud computing and microservice architectures, software operations fault diagnosis increasingly depends on runtime data such as logs and monitoring metrics. Large language models are effective at text understanding and summarization, but direct use in operations analysis still suffers from inadequate data organization, insufficient domain knowledge, and unstable outputs. The core problem addressed in this thesis is how to organize logs and monitoring metrics collaboratively so as to enhance large language models in analyzing operations faults and improve the stability and traceability of diagnostic results.

To address this problem, this thesis proposes a software operations fault diagnosis method based on large language models and collaborative analysis of logs and monitoring metrics. The method is centered on a large-language-model-based diagnostic framework. Logs are organized through time-window filtering, request correlation, and anomaly summarization, while monitoring metrics are organized through anomaly identification and textual abstraction. The two data sources are then unified into a shared analysis context. On this basis, retrieval augmentation is used to inject domain knowledge for knowledge-dependent scenarios, and structured reasoning is used to constrain analysis steps and result expression.

To evaluate the proposed method, a research validation prototype is implemented and an experimental framework is constructed with controlled scenarios as the main evidence and public samples as supplementary evidence. The main experiment contains 21 controlled samples from 7 fault families and adopts progressive comparisons covering single-source input, collaborative log-metric input, and enhanced variants. The results show that collaborative log-metric input clearly outperforms single-source input, retrieval augmentation further improves middleware- and database-related scenarios and raises Top-1 root-cause accuracy to 85.7\%, and structured reasoning increases output compliance from 66.7\% to 95.2\%. These results indicate that the proposed method can support software operations fault diagnosis more steadily in the controlled setting of this thesis, while complex fault propagation scenarios and larger-scale real-world validation remain open for future work.
