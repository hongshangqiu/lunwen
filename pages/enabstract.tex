% English Abstract

With the widespread adoption of cloud computing and microservices, modern software systems have become larger and more complex. Operations fault diagnosis faces many challenges, including scattered knowledge, heterogeneous data, and complex reasoning processes. Large language models show potential in natural language processing, but they still suffer from insufficient domain knowledge, lack of real-time context, and unstable results in operations scenarios. To address these issues, this paper proposes an auxiliary method for operations fault diagnosis, combining retrieval augmentation, dual-source evidence fusion, and structured reasoning.

First, this paper builds a knowledge base for operations scenarios and designs a document chunking strategy based on document structure, along with metadata filtering for retrieval, to improve knowledge acquisition efficiency.

Second, this paper designs a dual-source evidence fusion method that organizes log summaries and abnormal metrics into a structured context, and uses time windows and TraceID for log correlation, providing more comprehensive diagnostic evidence.

Third, this paper designs a structured prompt-based reasoning process that guides the model to output diagnosis results following unified steps, improving the interpretability of the diagnosis process.

To verify the proposed method, this paper implements a prototype system and conducts experiments on 7 typical fault scenarios. Experimental results show that knowledge retrieval augmentation improves accuracy from 29\% to 43\%; after fusing dual-source real-time evidence, accuracy further increases to 71\%; and the complete method reaches 86\%. Additionally, Top-3 accuracy reaches 93\% and evidence coverage reaches 79\%. These results indicate that the method can improve fault diagnosis accuracy in controlled experimental settings.
