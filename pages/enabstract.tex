% 英文摘要

With the widespread adoption of cloud computing and microservices, modern software systems have become increasingly large and complex. Traditional operations and maintenance methods face challenges in efficiently handling massive logs and monitoring metrics. To explore the application of large language models in operational fault diagnosis, this thesis proposes a set of fault diagnosis methods based on retrieval-augmented generation and verifies their feasibility through a prototype system.

The proposed methods mainly include three aspects. First, a knowledge base augmentation method for operations scenarios is constructed. Based on the ChromaDB vector database, a knowledge base incorporating standard operating procedures and historical fault cases is designed. A document chunking strategy based on Markdown structure is proposed, achieving vectorized storage and semantic retrieval of operations documents, supporting metadata filtering by service name and fault type. Second, a multi-source operations data fusion method is proposed. By designing a log correlation mechanism based on TraceID, retrieved results, discrete logs, and sequential monitoring metrics are organized into a unified structured analysis context for subsequent analysis. Third, a large language model-based root cause analysis method is designed. Through structured prompt templates, the model is guided to analyze in the logical sequence of "fault summary --- evidence analysis --- root cause inference --- repair suggestions". Meanwhile, a sandbox instruction interception mechanism is introduced to simulate and verify the generated operations commands, ensuring the security of the analysis process.

To evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, a prototype system is built and seven typical fault test scenarios with four sets of comparative experiments are designed. Experimental results demonstrate that relying solely on the LLM for direct responses results in an accuracy of approximately 35\%. Introducing RAG-based knowledge augmentation improves this to 48\%; further integrating real-time logs and monitoring metrics increases it to 63\%; and the complete method achieves 79\% accuracy with 93\% Top-3 accuracy and 68\% evidence coverage rate. The experimental results validate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, providing a reference for intelligent operations and maintenance.
