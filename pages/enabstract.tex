% English Abstract

With the adoption of cloud computing and microservice architectures, software systems have become increasingly complex, making fault diagnosis in software operations more difficult. Logs and monitoring metrics are the two most important evidence sources in operations scenarios: logs record discrete abnormal events, while metrics reflect continuous runtime states. However, log-only analysis often lacks trend information, and metric-only analysis is usually insufficient for locating concrete abnormal events. Therefore, how to collaboratively use logs and metrics for fault diagnosis becomes a key research problem.

To address this problem, this paper proposes a software operations fault diagnosis method based on collaborative analysis of logs and monitoring metrics. The core of the method is a dual-source diagnosis pipeline that correlates logs through time windows and TraceID, detects abnormal metrics, converts metric changes into textual descriptions, and organizes both sources into a unified analysis context. On top of this core pipeline, retrieval augmentation is introduced as an auxiliary knowledge enhancement mechanism to compensate for domain knowledge gaps, while structured reasoning is introduced as an auxiliary output constraint to improve interpretability and comparability.

To evaluate the proposed method, a controlled prototype system is implemented and five comparative settings are designed across seven typical fault scenarios: Metric-Only, Log-Only, Log+Metric, Log+Metric+RAG, and Full-Method. Experimental results show that the root-cause accuracy is 29\% for Metric-Only and 43\% for Log-Only, while the collaborative Log+Metric method improves the accuracy to 71\%. After adding retrieval augmentation, the accuracy further increases to 86\%. Structured reasoning brings limited additional gains in accuracy, but improves evidence coverage from 74\% to 83\% and output compliance from 64\% to 95\% while maintaining the same 86\% accuracy. These results indicate that a research line centered on log-metric collaborative diagnosis, with retrieval augmentation and structured reasoning as auxiliary modules, is effective for controlled software operations fault diagnosis.
