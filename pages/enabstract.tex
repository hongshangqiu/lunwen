% English Abstract

With the widespread adoption of cloud computing and microservices, modern software systems have become larger and more complex. Traditional operations methods face many difficulties in fault diagnosis, including scattered knowledge, different types of data, and complex reasoning processes. Large language models have shown potential in this area, but they still have problems such as lacking domain-specific knowledge, missing real-time information, and giving unstable results.

This paper proposes an auxiliary method for software operations fault diagnosis that combines retrieval-augmented generation, dual-source evidence fusion, and structured reasoning.

First, this paper builds a knowledge base for operations scenarios and designs a document chunking strategy based on Markdown structure, along with metadata filtering for retrieval. This addresses the problem that operations knowledge is scattered and hard to use.

Second, this paper designs a dual-source evidence fusion method that organizes log summaries and abnormal metrics into a structured context. This addresses the problem that logs and monitoring metrics are in different formats and hard to use directly for reasoning.

Third, this paper designs a structured prompt-based analysis method that guides the large language model through the process of fault summary, evidence analysis, cause inference, and repair suggestions. This addresses the problems of unstable results and poor traceability in root cause analysis.

To verify the effectiveness of the proposed method, this paper builds a prototype system and conducts experiments on 7 typical fault scenarios with 4 comparison groups. Experimental results show that using the large language model alone achieves about 29\% accuracy. After adding knowledge retrieval, accuracy improves to 43\%. With real-time logs and monitoring metrics added, accuracy reaches 71\%. The complete method achieves 86\% accuracy, with 93\% Top-3 accuracy and 79\% evidence coverage rate. These results show that combining retrieval augmentation, dual-source evidence fusion, and structured reasoning can improve the accuracy of large language models in software operations fault diagnosis, providing a reproducible implementation path for LLM-assisted operations analysis.
