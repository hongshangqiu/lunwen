With the widespread adoption of cloud computing and microservices, modern software systems have become increasingly large and complex. Traditional operations and maintenance methods face challenges in efficiently handling massive logs and monitoring metrics. To explore the application of large language models in operational fault diagnosis, this thesis designs and implements an LLM-based intelligent fault diagnosis prototype system for software operations --- LLM-Ops Copilot.

The main contributions of this thesis include three aspects. First, a domain-specific knowledge base for software operations is constructed. Based on the ChromaDB vector database, the knowledge base incorporates standard operating procedures and historical fault cases. A document chunking strategy based on Markdown structure is designed, achieving vectorized storage and semantic retrieval of operations documents, supporting metadata filtering by service name and fault type. Second, a multi-source operations data fusion method is proposed. By designing a log correlation mechanism based on TraceID, retrieved results, discrete logs, and sequential monitoring metrics are organized into a unified structured analysis context for subsequent analysis. Third, an LLM-based Root Cause Analysis (RCA) engine is designed and implemented. A structured prompt template including role definition, context specification, reasoning constraints, and output format is designed, guiding the model to analyze in the logical sequence of "fault summary --- evidence analysis --- root cause inference --- repair suggestions". To prevent security risks from operations commands generated by the model, the system introduces a sandbox instruction interception mechanism to simulate and verify the generated commands.

To evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method, this thesis designs seven typical fault test scenarios and four sets of comparative experiments. Experimental results demonstrate that relying solely on the LLM for direct responses results in an accuracy of approximately 43\%. Introducing RAG-based knowledge augmentation improves this to 57\%; further integrating real-time logs and monitoring metrics increases it to 71\%; and the complete method achieves 86\% accuracy with 100\% Top-3 accuracy and 78\% evidence coverage rate. The experimental results validate the role of multi-source data fusion and structured prompting in improving diagnosis effectiveness, providing a reference for intelligent operations and maintenance.
