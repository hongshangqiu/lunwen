    {\small With the widespread adoption of cloud computing, microservices, and containerization, modern software
     systems have grown increasingly large and complex, posing significant challenges to traditional operations and
     maintenance workflows that rely heavily on manual expertise. To improve fault diagnosis efficiency and
     operational intelligence, this thesis designs and implements an LLM-based intelligent fault diagnosis prototype
     system for software operations --- LLM-Ops Copilot.}

   {\small First, a domain-specific knowledge base for software operations is constructed. Based on the ChromaDB
     vector database, the knowledge base incorporates standard operating procedures (SOP) and historical fault cases. A document chunking strategy based on Markdown structure and a hybrid retrieval method
     are designed, achieving vectorized storage and semantic retrieval of operations documents, supporting metadata filtering by service name and fault type. Second, a multi-source operations data fusion method is proposed. A spatiotemporal alignment mechanism based on TraceID is designed to extract anomaly metric features and
     integrate retrieved results, discrete logs, and sequential monitoring metrics into a unified structured analysis context. Third, an LLM-based Root Cause Analysis (RCA) engine is designed and
     implemented. A structured prompt template including role definition, context specification, reasoning constraints, and output format is designed, guiding the model to analyze in the
     logical sequence of "fault summary --- evidence analysis --- root cause inference --- repair suggestions". At the same time, the system introduces a sandbox instruction interception mechanism to safely identify and simulate the execution of operations commands generated by the model, realizing a closed-loop verification of fault diagnosis while ensuring system safety. Finally, seven typical fault scenarios and four sets of comparative experiments are
     designed to evaluate the effectiveness of the method.}

    {\small Experimental results demonstrate that relying solely on the LLM for direct responses leads to
     significant limitations in domain expertise, with an accuracy of only 43\%. Introducing RAG-based knowledge
     augmentation improves this to 57\%; further integrating real-time logs and monitoring metrics increases it to
     71\%; and the complete method achieves 86\% accuracy with 100\% Top-3 accuracy and 78\% evidence coverage rate.
     Typical case analysis validates the critical role of evidence fusion and structured prompting in improving
     diagnosis quality, providing a new technical pathway for intelligent operations and maintenance.}
